Website Redesign Requirements Document

1. Project Overview

Julie’s Family Learning Program (JFLP) seeks to redesign its current WordPress website to better serve its core audiences, improve usability, enhance storytelling, and support fundraising and outreach efforts. The current site is outdated, difficult to manage, and lacks integration with internal systems.

2. Target Audiences & Personas

Donors
Individual and corporate/foundation donors
Wishlist donors (food, clothes, equipment)
Adult Learners
Ages 16+, seeking high school equivalency via ABE or FD programs
Referral Partners
Organizations referring adult learners
Volunteers
Tutors, interns, corporate groups
Community Members
Alumni, Boston Public School parents, workforce development participants
3. Program Areas & Web Pages

Adult Education
ABE and FD program details
Intake process and interest form
Children’s Services
Early childhood education (3 months – 5 years)
Boston Pre-K partnership
Workforce Development
ESOL, digital literacy, alumni services
4. User Journeys & Lifecycle

Donors
Discover via events, social media, or outreach
Donate via website → receive automated email → manual database entry → hard copy thank-you
Adult Learners
Mostly in-person or phone/text intake
Small percentage via embedded website form
Volunteers
Discover via website or college partnerships
Submit interest form → contacted by Volunteer Coordinator
5. Website Goals

Primary Goals
Fundraising
Outreach to prospective students and referral sources
Secondary Goals
Volunteer coordination
Storytelling and engagement
Integration with CRM and internal systems
6. Communication Ecosystem

Channels
Social Media: Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube (1–2 posts/week)
Email: E-news, appeals, event invites (1–4/month)
Print: Direct mail, gala invitations, annual reports
Segmentation
Minimal segmentation; most communications go to full list
Direct communication for enrolled students and volunteers
7. Technical & Platform Requirements

Current Platform: WordPress
Desired Features
Easy-to-update CMS
CRM integration (DonorPerfect or similar)
Embedded and accessible interest forms
Improved image handling and design layout
Mobile responsiveness
Design Preferences
Empowering, modern, clean aesthetic
Student stories and virtual donor tour
Canva-compatible assets
Staff Capacity
Limited: ~2 hours/week
No full-time communications staff
Trainable staff with limited technical expertise
External Support
Currently using a consultant (technical but not design-focused)
Open to replacing consultant
8. Analytics & Reporting

Current Use: No analytics in place
Desired Capabilities
Basic website performance metrics
Donation tracking
Form submission tracking
Recommendations welcome
9. Design & Collaboration Tools

Preferred Tools
Microsoft Office 365
Canva
Not Used
Figma (not currently part of workflow)
10. Success Metrics

Website
Increased donor retention and acquisition
Improved user experience and accessibility
Higher form submission rates
Programs
Adult Learner outcomes tracked via LACES
Children’s Services tracked via educator assessments and parent reviews
